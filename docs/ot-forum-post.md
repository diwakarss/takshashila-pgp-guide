# PGP Guide — a study companion I built for our cohort 📚🧭

Hi everyone! I've been building something for us: **PGP Guide**, a desktop app that turns everything we get each week — webinar recordings, learning units, readings, assignments — into a private, searchable study companion on your own computer.

I built it because our material is scattered across recordings, PDFs, and email — and because I wanted AI help that respects how we're supposed to learn. One rule everywhere: **the AI researches, coaches, and quizzes — you think, decide, and write.**

*(screenshots below each section)*

- **🎓 Tutor** — ask anything; answers cite the actual class transcripts, learning units, and readings, with hand-drawn concept illustrations.
- **✍️ Quiz** — per-course quizzes from what was actually covered; tracks scores, streaks, and weak spots.
- **🔍 Research** — web research for assignments, with structured lenses and cited sources.
- **📓 Notebook** — highlight any answer to capture a note; notes keep their citations.
- **📁 Projects** — assignments arrive automatically with briefs and due dates, and open as guided step-by-step flows built on Takshashila's own [scholar framework](https://github.com/TakshashilaInst/scholar-web). The coach helps at every step; the writing is yours alone, and exports carry the required disclaimers.

**It stays current:** new webinars are transcribed into clean transcripts + study notes each week, and learning units and assignments sync from the hub — a "N new" pill appears, one click to update. IR LU-01–03 are already in.

**Your AI, your choice:** the wizard connects a Claude/ChatGPT account you already have, an API key, or a fully local model (Ollama). **Private by design:** everything lives on your machine, powered by a local brain based on [gbrain](https://www.npmjs.com/package/gbrain) — no accounts, no cloud copies of your notes.

**Get it:** installers on the [Releases page](https://github.com/diwakarss/takshashila-pgp-guide/releases) (macOS Apple Silicon + Windows). Both are unsigned for now — macOS: right-click → Open; Windows: *More info → Run anyway*. First run downloads the course library and indexes it locally (~15–40 min, one-time); Windows auto-updates after that.
